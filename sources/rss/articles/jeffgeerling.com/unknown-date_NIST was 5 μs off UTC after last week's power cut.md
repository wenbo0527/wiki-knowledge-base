# NIST was 5 μs off UTC after last week's power cut

> 来源: jeffgeerling.com  
> 发布时间: Mon, 22 Dec 2025 16:28:05 +0000  
> 分类: 海外技术博客  
> 优先级: medium

## 摘要

<p>If you were 5 microseconds late today, blame it on NIST.</p>
<p>Their facility in Boulder Colorado just had its power cut for multiple days. After a backup generator failed, their main ensemble clock lost track of UTC, or Universal Time Coordinated.</p>
<p>But even if you used the <a href="https://tf.nist.gov/tf-cgi/servers.cgi">NTP timing servers they run</a>, they were never off by more than 5 microseconds.</p>
<p>5 μs might seem insignificant. But it <em>is</em> significant for scientists and universities who rely on NIST's more <a href="https://wsts.atis.org/wp-content/uploads/2023/03/04-Judah-Levine.The-NIST-Fiber-Based-Time-Service.pdf">specialized timing signals</a>.</p>
