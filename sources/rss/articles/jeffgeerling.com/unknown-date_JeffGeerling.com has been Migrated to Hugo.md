## 摘要

<p>Since 2009, this website has <a href="https://www.jeffgeerling.com/blog/2009/moved-drupal-hello-drupal">run on Drupal</a>. Starting with Drupal 6, and progressing through major site upgrades and migrations to 7, 8, 9, and 10, I used the site as a way to dogfood the same CMS (Content Management System) I used in my day job for <em>over a decade</em>.</p>
<figure class="insert-image"><img alt="JeffGeerling.com - Old Drupal site" height="auto" src="https://www.jeffgeerling.com/blog/2026/migrated-to-hugo/jeffgeerling-old-drupal.png" width="700" />
</figure>

<p>But as time progressed—especially after completing a <a href="https://www.jeffgeerling.com/blog/2020/migrating-jeffgeerlingcom-drupal-8-live">grueling upgrade from Drupal 7 to 8</a>—my enthusiasm for maintaining what's now a more enterprise-focused <a href="https://www.acquia.com/blog/what-digital-experience-platform-dxp">Digital Experience Platform</a> or 'DXP' for a personal blog has waned.</p>
